The record

Fifty-nine years on the same corner

Since 1966, Mano has been the beating heart of Bourj Hammoud. Three generations of one family curing basterma, hanging soujouk, and shaving shawarma off the spit the way it was always done. One street corner, one recipe book — and half of Beirut swearing it's the best sandwich in Lebanon.

Timeline
1966

Mano opens on a street corner in Bourj Hammoud. The basterma is cured in-house from day one.

1980s

The soujouk recipe survives everything Beirut throws at it. The line outside doesn't get shorter.

2000s

A second generation takes the knife. Mano Bakery and Mano Burger join the family.

TODAY

Three generations in. Half of Beirut still swears it's the best sandwich in Lebanon.
