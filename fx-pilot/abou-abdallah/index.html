The house

Breakfast before the city wakes

Abou Abdallah opens at six because Dawra wakes up hungry. Inside, the furniture and decor carry you back decades, Fairuz plays softly, and clay bowls of foul and fetteh land steaming on wooden tables. Reviewers call it the best foul and fetteh in Lebanon; the regulars just call it morning.

Everything is slow-cooked the way it was taught — and finished before mid-afternoon, when the pots are empty and the doors close until tomorrow's dawn.

The morning ritual

Three movements, performed daily

movement 1

Fatteh, drowning in ghee

Toasted bread, warm chickpeas, yogurt, and the golden pour that makes it famous.

movement 2

Eggs in the clay dish

Egg pottery with kawarma or sausages — served bubbling.

movement 3

Msabaha & balila

Slow-simmered, generous, finished with the good olive oil.
